Environment

Unfortunately the human population of Madagascar suffer from extreme poverty and poor living conditions.

They rely heavily on slash and burn farming techniques to grow high value crops, such as cocoa beans and tobacco, to sell in order to feed themselves.

Slash and burn farming is devastating to both flora and fauna.

Since the arrival of humans around 2,350 years ago, Madagascar has lost more than 90 percent of its original forest.
